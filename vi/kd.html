Vinaya Pitaka - Monastic Rules

Bhikkhu Khandaka

  • Translated from the Pali by Ebo Croffie (coming sooner or later).

The Khandakas are often arranged into two parts - The Mahāvagga (Khandakas 1-10) and the Cullavagga (Khandakas 11-22).

This arrangement seems to be largely one of convenience so that there are two volumes of manageable size rather than a single huge volume. Both arrangements are included below.

(Mahāvagga)

(Mv1.)     Khandaka 1. Ordination
(Mv2.)     Khandaka 2. The Uposatha
(Mv3.)     Khandaka 3. Rains Residence
(Mv4.)     Khandaka 4. Pavarana (Invitation)
(Mv5.)     Khandaka 5. Footwear
(Mv6.)     Khandaka 6. Medicine
(Mv7.)     Khandaka 7. The Kathina
(Mv8.)     Khandaka 8. Robe Cloth
(Mv9.)     Khandaka 9. Principles for Community Transactions
(Mv10.)     Khandaka 10. Unamimity in the Community

(Cullavagga)

(Cv1.)     Khandaka 11. Disciplinary Transactions
(Cv2.)     Khandaka 12. Penance and Probation
(Cv3.)     Khandaka 13. Imposing Penance and Probation
(Cv4.)     Khandaka 14. Settling Issues
(Cv5.)     Khandaka 15. Miscellany
(Cv6.)     Khandaka 16. Lodgings
(Cv7.)     Khandaka 17. Schism
(Cv8.)     Khandaka 18. Protocols
(Cv9.)     Khandaka 19. Cancelling the Pātimokkha
(Cv10.)     Khandaka 20. bhikkhunis
(Cv11.)     Khandaka 21. The First Counsel
(Cv12.)     Khandaka 22. The Second Counsel
